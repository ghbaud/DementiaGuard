Scam Detection Tools

There are tools that can watch for threats in the background -- scanning messages, emails, and websites for signs of scam activity. They're not perfect, but they add a valuable layer of protection, especially when you can't be there in person.

Things Worth Considering

  • Installing a scam detection app on their phone
  • Adding a browser extension that flags suspicious websites
  • Enabling phone-level call and text blocking
  • Setting up a trusted contact who gets alerts

What it does: Monitors messages, texts, and emails for suspicious patterns. Sends real-time alerts when potential scams are detected. Includes educational quizzes and simulated scam texts to build awareness.

Platforms: iOS app, Chrome browser extension

Cost: Free basic plan (alerts, quizzes, tips). Premium $3.99/month (unlimited scam tests, full resources, priority support).

How to install: Download from the App Store (search "SeniorShield.AI") or add the Chrome extension from the Chrome Web Store.

Strengths: Real-time detection; the fake scam text feature is clever -- it sends simulated scams and uses failures as teaching moments. One caregiver reported their family member with early-onset dementia went from replacing their credit card 12+ times to significantly fewer incidents.

Limitations: Requires consistent use of the monitored channels. Won't catch scams that come through platforms it doesn't monitor.

What it does: A browser extension that actively blocks dangerous websites, phishing pages, fake download buttons, malicious redirects, tech support scams, and impersonation sites -- in real time, across all tabs. Unlike tools that scan messages after the fact, Guardio prevents the browser from loading the dangerous page in the first place.

Platforms: Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari (browser extension)

Cost: Free basic scan. Premium plans start around $9/month (covers up to 5 family members).

How to install: Visit guard.io and install the browser extension.

Strengths: Works across all tabs automatically -- no action needed from the user. Blocks threats before they load rather than alerting after. Family plan allows caregivers to monitor protection status remotely. Particularly strong against fake login pages and tech support scam popups.

Limitations: Only protects browsing -- doesn't monitor text messages, phone calls, or apps outside the browser. The premium price is higher than some alternatives.

Why it's different: Most tools on this page detect scams in messages or emails. Guardio blocks dangerous websites themselves. These are complementary -- using both a message scanner (like SeniorShield) and a browser blocker (like Guardio) covers more ground.

guard.io

Last reviewed: March 2026

What it does: A Chrome extension that performs real-time AI-based scanning of every website visited. It checks URLs against a global threat database and uses AI to identify recently created phishing sites that haven't been flagged yet by traditional blocklists.

Platforms: Chrome (browser extension)

Cost: Free extension with basic scanning

How to install: Search "Criminal IP" in the Chrome Web Store, or visit the Chrome Web Store listing.

Strengths: Particularly good at catching brand-new phishing sites that are only hours old -- before they appear on traditional blocklists. Free to use. Runs quietly in the background.

Limitations: Chrome only. Focused on website threats -- doesn't scan messages, emails, or social media content. Less user-friendly interface than Guardio.

Why it matters: As scammers increasingly use AI to write convincing messages, knowing the signs of AI-generated text can help you evaluate suspicious communications.

Why This Matters

Online scams are becoming dramatically more sophisticated. Scammers now use AI to clone the voices of loved ones, create deepfake video calls, and write perfectly convincing emails. The scale is staggering -- elder fraud losses exceeded $3.4 billion in 2023, and AI is making it worse.

These tools can't catch everything, but they reduce the attack surface. Think of them as a smoke detector -- they won't prevent every fire, but they give you a chance to respond before real damage is done.
